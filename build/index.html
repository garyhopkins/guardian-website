My insurance company gave me a settlement rather quickly, should I accept it?
Following a disaster, the insurance company may propose a prompt settlement, but caution is advised. This amount may not adequately reimburse you and could fall significantly short of what you rightfully deserve.
What does a public insurance adjuster do?
A public insurance adjuster works solely for you, not the insurance company, and serves as your expert in managing loss adjustments. Their role involves aiding you in the preparation, submission, and adjustment of insurance claims following a covered loss. They perform inventories, facilitate appraisals, and handle the aspects of the policy that directly affect you. This often entails various steps that you may have limited knowledge of, yet can substantially impact the final settlement amount.
Why should I hire a public insurance adjuster?
While insurance companies prioritize their profits, it's important to note that the adjusters they employ often face heavy workloads and insufficient compensation. Due to time constraints, many company adjusters may not thoroughly assess everything you are rightfully entitled to. Furthermore, adjusters directly employed by insurance companies may have incentives tied to their earnings, raises, potential promotions, and pressure from supervisors to minimize expenses whenever possible. In contrast, a public insurance adjuster will diligently collaborate with you to meticulously prepare and present your claim to the insurance company, always prioritizing your best interests over those of the insurance company.
Doesn't my insurance company provide me with an experienced and licensed adjuster?
While one might assume otherwise, it's essential not to rely solely on this assumption. Especially in today's climate, where numerous storms have increased the demand for adjusters, there's a possibility that your assigned adjuster may have limited prior experience in handling insurance claims. Surprisingly, insurance company adjusters are not required to undergo comprehensive education or licensing procedures. They can transition from unrelated professions, like shoe salesmen, to insurance adjusters in a short span of time. The current high demand for adjusters means that sometimes any available person is assigned to the role. To ensure your peace of mind, consider inquiring about your adjuster's experience, training, and licensing. (Note: No offense intended towards shoe salesmen; my preference lies in having a qualified professional handle my claim!)
What is a public adjusters fee for their services?
The public insurance adjuster's fee is a percentage of the insurance company's settlement with you. By seeking to maximize your settlement, your public insurance adjuster's service can save you the cost of their fees.
